### Schema of Situational Awareness Categories

1. **caution_advice** — Tweets giving warnings, advice, preparation, or safety tips.
   *Examples:* “Stay away from downtown Napa until authorities confirm it’s safe.”, “Stocking up on water and batteries before aftershocks hit.”

2. **affected_people** — Mentions of people injured, missing, trapped, dead, or emotional distress.
   *Examples:* “My neighbours are injured.”, “Hope everyone in Napa is safe.”

3. **residential_infrastructure** — Mentions of damage to homes, personal property, public infrastructure, or utilities.
   *Examples:* “Our roof collapsed.”, “Power lines are down.”, “The main bridge is closed.”

4. **needs_donations** — Mentions of requests for help, calls for volunteers, or donation/relief activities.
   *Examples:* "Need blankets and clean water in downtown.", "Red Cross collecting donations."

5. **response_recovery** — Mentions of emergency response, cleanup, repair, or official action.
   *Examples:* "Firefighters rescuing people from collapsed buildings.", "Volunteers helping rebuild homes."

6. **weather_environment** — Mentions of physical or environmental phenomena such as shaking, aftershocks, ground motion, landslides, dust, or noise.
   *Examples:* "Felt another aftershock just now.", "The ground was moving like waves."

7. **none** — The tweet does not contain any situational awareness information or does not fit the above categories.
   *Examples:* "Just finished breakfast.", "Earthquakes are scary but life goes on."
